Florida State University’s Association for Computing Machinery student chapter is a Registered Student Organization serving all FSU students. We strive to enrich each member’s time at FSU, while promoting computing as a science and profession. Our membership is comprised of both undergraduate, and graduate students with a diverse range of interests, skills, and experience levels.

Open Source Software Group

Our OSSG assists those interested in open source development to collaborate on projects of personal interest. This group is open to developers of all experience levels, and receives guidance from Dr. Sonia Haiduc. Check out our events page for meeting dates and times.

Programming Club

Our Programming Club is intended for programmers who want to practice implementing algorithms quickly and efficiently, along with those who have an interest in joining our ACM International Collegiate Programming Contest team. More information is available on the Programming Club site.

Programming Contest

The semesterly ACM Programming Contest is a competition open to students and non students. Teams of up to three people have five hours to solve a packet of questions varying in topic and difficulty. To register or learn more about the contest, check out our contest site.
